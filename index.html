About Me

I'm Arthur Loureiro, a researcher interested in Observational Cosmology! 
My research focus is Large Scale Structure of the Universe, Cosmic Shear,Fourier analysis of Galaxy Surveys, Neutrinos in Cosmology, Bayesian Methods applied to Observational Cosmology, and cross-correlations between photometric and spectroscopic surveys. 

In other words, I am a data scientist with a strong passion for Cosmological data.

Currently, I am an active member of the Euclid Consortium and the Kilo Degree Survey

Neutrino Astrophysics

Probing neutrino physics with Cosmological Surveys

Using various cosmological data sets, ranging from Cosmic Microwave Background to the largest spectroscopic galaxy survey to date, my interest is to probe and study neutrino physics from a cosmological perspective. Recently, I have lead a study to obtain constraints for the lightest neutrino mass and the sum of neutrino mass species with a combination of particle physics constraints and cosmological probes.


We found the lightest neutrino to be at least 0.086 eV (95% CI)

In the media:
"Lightest neutrino is at least 6 million times lighter than an electron" - Nature 
"Mysterious Neutrinos Get New Mass Estimate" - Scientific American

Confirmation of Time

"Confirmation of time" is a piece produced by the artist Franziska Hatton in collaboration with me for the "If a Tree Falls in the Forest" Exhibition at the Grizedale Forest. The workpiece explores the concepts of entropy using the forest materials to construct an interpretive model of the expanding Universe and our perception of time. 
This is the result of a 9 months long collaboration between artists from The Royal Academy of Arts and Cosmologists from University College London.

Date: November 2018
Collaborators: Franziska Hatton
Category: Cosmology, Arts, Collaborations, Entropy
