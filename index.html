About Me

Hello! I'm Arthur Loureiro, a PhD Candidate studying Observational Cosmology at University College London. My research focus is Large Scale Structure of the Universe, Fourier analysis of Galaxy Surveys, Neutrinos in Cosmology, Bayesian Methods applied to Observational Cosmology, and cross-correlations between photometric and spectroscopic surveys.
In other words, I am a data scientist with a strong passion for Cosmological data.
Currently, I am an active member of the Dark Energy Survey and the Euclid Consortium.



When I'm not using images of the sky to study the fundamental physics of our Universe, I'm using my (much smaller) camera to take pictures of the streets around me.

ZXCorr

Cross-Correlation between Cosmological Fields in Harmonic Space

The ZXCorr project uses cross-correlations between cosmological fields to obtain accurate and precise cosmological measurements from galaxy surveys and other cosmological probes. Recently, we produced outstanding results from a tomographic analysis of BOSS DR12 data, measuring the equation-of-state of Dark Energy with ~ 4% accuracy.

  • Members: Arthur Loureiro, B. Moraes, F. B. Abdalla, A. Cuceu, M. McLeod, L. Whiteway, S. T. Balan, A. Benoit-Lévy, O. Lahav, M. Manera, R. Rollins, H. S. Xavier
  • Publications: ArXiv:1809.07204
  • Category: Large Scale Structure, Observational Cosmology

Neutrino Astrophysics

Probing neutrino properties with Cosmological Surveys

Using various cosmological data sets, ranging from Cosmic Microwave Background to the largest spectroscopic galaxy survey to date, my interest is to probe and study neutrino physics from a cosmological perspective. Recently, I have lead a study to obtain constraints for the lightest neutrino mass and the sum of neutrino mass species with a combination of particle physics constraints and cosmological probes. More details soon.

  • Category: Large Scale Structure, Observational Cosmology, Particle Physics, Neutrinos

Confirmation of Time

If a Tree Falls in the Forest

"Confirmation of time" is a piece produced by the artist Franziska Hatton in collaboration with me for the "If a Tree Falls in the Forest" Exhibition at the Grizedale Forest. The workpiece explores the concepts of entropy using the forest materials to construct an interpretive model of the expanding Universe and our perception of time.
This is the result of a 9 months long collaboration between artists from The Royal Academy of Arts and Cosmologists from University College London.

  • Date: November 2018
  • Category: Cosmology, Arts, Collaborations, Entropy

Daydream XI's Next Album

Scientific consultancy for the prog-metal band Daydream XI's next album.

In 2016, Tiago Masseti – Daydream XI's front-man, lead singer, and guitar player – approach me with ideas for their next album: a sci-fi epic telling the whole story of a Universe created by an Artificial Inteligence. Tiago and I brainstormed ideas and forth. As the songs and lyrics were developed, my job was to ensure that the scientific concepts presented in the album were accurate and possible. We took main inspiration in ideas presented by Prof. Penrose for an eternal cyclical Universe. Daydream XI incorporated cosmological concepts such as the formation of structure and the increase of entropy as the arrow of time in their songs by using increasingly complex time signatures.

Tiago's lyrics tell the story of the Universe, from beginning to the formation of first structures, the first advanced civilizations, and the challenges imposed by artificial intelligence and machine learning in years to come.

  • Release Date: Stay tunned.
  • Category: Cosmology, Prog-Metal, Art, Collaborations
